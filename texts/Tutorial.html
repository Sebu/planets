Aristotle says that in each system of a planetary star, except for the moon's system, one needs 'unwinding' spheres, one less in number than the spheres of the system. It is crucial to this account that each sphere only affects the position of the poles of the next sphere below. In this discussion, we shall look at a planetary system of type of Jupiter or Saturn to see how the unwinders work and why they are one less than the number of 'winding' spheres.
